# Jatin Dehmiwal

**Robot psychology · Machine behaviour · Social robotics**

I am building an open, experimental practice around one question:

> How can we measure, explain, and improve the behaviour of robots and AI agents without pretending that they are human?

My long-term goal is to work as a **robot psychologist**: a researcher-engineer who studies artificial behaviour at the level of individual agents, human–robot interaction, and multi-agent systems.

Here, *robot psychology* means the scientific and engineering study of artificial agents. It does not mean human clinical psychology or automated mental-health treatment.

## What I study

- **Machine behaviour:** consistency, adaptation, calibration, goal stability, social influence, and behavioural drift.
- **Cognitive and social robotics:** memory, turn-taking, trust, personalization, coordination, and interaction with people.
- **Affective computing:** emotion-related signals treated as uncertain observations, not as direct access to a person's inner state.
- **Artificial societies:** cooperation, norms, conflict, polarization, information spread, and collective behaviour.
- **Responsible deployment:** reproducibility, privacy, human oversight, failure analysis, and clear limits on claims.

## How I work

1. State a behavioural hypothesis before building the system.
2. Define observable variables, controls, baselines, and failure conditions.
3. Run repeated trials instead of presenting a single impressive example.
4. Report uncertainty, negative results, and behavioural changes across versions.
5. Separate observed behaviour from claims about consciousness, emotion, personality, or intent.

## Current research roadmap

| Project | Central question | Intended evidence |
|---|---|---|
| `machine-behaviour-lab` | How stable is an AI agent under paraphrasing, pressure, memory changes, and conflicting goals? | Repeated behavioural tests, baselines, confidence intervals, and versioned reports |
| `social-robot-experiments` | How should a robot adapt its timing, distance, gaze, speech, and turn-taking around people? | ROS 2 simulation, controlled conditions, interaction logs, and measurable outcomes |
| `artificial-society-lab` | How do artificial agents form norms, cooperate, spread information, and polarize? | Multi-agent experiments, multiple random seeds, intervention studies, and sensitivity analysis |
| `robot-psychology-reading-list` | What knowledge already exists across machine behaviour, HRI, cognitive robotics, affective computing, and AI safety? | A maintained taxonomy with papers, datasets, tools, replications, and open questions |

## Questions guiding the work

- Can an artificial agent have a reproducible behavioural profile without treating it like a human personality?
- Which behaviours persist when prompts, environments, memories, tools, or model versions change?
- When does apparent empathy represent useful adaptation, and when is it merely persuasive imitation?
- How can robots express uncertainty and recover trust after making mistakes?
- Which individual agent traits create cooperation, manipulation, conformity, or instability at population scale?
